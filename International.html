PR Visa

A PR (Permanent Residency) visa is a type of immigration status that allows individuals to live and work in a foreign country on a permanent basis. Unlike temporary visas, PR visas typically offer greater stability and long-term residency rights. Holders of a PR visa often enjoy benefits such as access to social services, healthcare, and education on par with citizens of the host country.In some countries, a permanent residency is granted upon investment in real estate, bonds, or infrastructure projects.

Study Visa

A study visa, also known as a student visa, is a type of visa that allows individuals to enter a foreign country for the purpose of pursuing education at a recognized educational institution. This visa is typically granted to foreign nationals who have been accepted into an academic program, such as a university or college, in the host country

Business Visa

A business visa is a type of visa that allows individuals to travel to a foreign country for business-related purposes. It is distinct from tourist visas, as it is specifically designed for activities such as attending meetings, conferences, negotiating business deals, and engaging in other professional or commercial activities. The requirements and privileges associated with business visas can vary significantly from one country to another.

Visitor Visa

A Visitor Visa, also known as a Tourist Visa or Temporary Resident Visa, is a type of visa that allows individuals to enter a foreign country for a temporary stay, typically for tourism, business, or visiting friends and family. The specific rules and requirements for obtaining a Visitor Visa vary from country to country.
